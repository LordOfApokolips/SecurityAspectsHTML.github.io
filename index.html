Malware

Malware is a piece of software designed to damage and/or control computer systems. Malware is a short term for malicious software. Malware is an inclusive term for all types of malicious software. Some examples include:
  • Adware - While some forms of adware may be considered legitimate, others make unauthorised access to computer systems and greatly disrupt users
  • Botnets – Short for “robot network,” these are networks of infected computers under the control of a single attacking partie using command-and-control servers. Botnets are highly versatile and adaptable.
  • Ransomware – Is a criminal business model that uses malicious software to hold valuable files, data or information for ransom. Victims of a ransomware attack may have their operations severely degraded or shut down entirely.
  • Spyware – Malware that collects information about the usage of the infected computer and communicates it back to the attacker. The term includes botnets, adware, backdoor behavior, key-loggers, data theft and net-worms.

Viruses and Hacking

A computer virus is a certain type of program for computers and requires the users input to build upon itself and affect anything. This replication of itself happens by changing other computer programs and inserting itself as code in the device's main code.
The primary difference between a virus and a worm is that viruses must be triggered by the activation of their host; whereas worms are stand-alone malicious programs that can self-replicate and propagate independently as soon as they have breached the system.

Hacking

Hacking is the process by which someone is trying to gain unauthorised access to a computer system or network.
There are two types of hackers:
  • Black hat hackers - Black hat hackers are people who access systems illegally with mailcious intent and is often for personal gain.

  • White hat hackers - White hat hackers are people who work with people and/or companies to help identify and find out about weaknesses in their systems and make updates to help make it better. They do this to ensure that black hat hackers cannot access the system's data illegally.

Protection against Malware and Viruses

There are many ways to be protected from these types of software. The main ways include:

Firewalls - Firewalls can be both software and hardware. Firewalls are packet filters, stateful inspection and proxy server firewalls.

Packet Filter Firewall - A packet filter firewall is one of the most simple firewalls. It applies its rules (A packet filter which is present in the firewall will check the source and destination IP address of the packet being sent. This is one of the many things it does to ensure safety of the receiving side.) to each packet and the allows or disallows that packet from the network.

Stateful Inspection - Stateful inspection, also known as dynamic packet filtering, collects data from every connection made. It keeps a list of all safe connections. When another connection is attempted, it will check the qualities to see if it is safe connection. It can inspect packets to see if they contain malicious code.

>Proxy server firewall - A proxy server is a gate between you and the internet. A good proxy server can keep the users on the network safe by filtering out unsafe content. It can help maintain your privacy because the proxy server will have a different IP address from you.

Anti Virus Software - Anti virus software is specifically designed to help detect, protect and remove and malicious software or viruses. Once it has been installed, most antivirus software run automatically in the background to provide real-time protection against virus attacks.
